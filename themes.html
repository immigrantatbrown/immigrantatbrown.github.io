Their Stories

One of the most interesting aspects of this project, personally, was the undeniably obvious recurrence of select themes across the spectrum of participant answers. I pinpointed these themes and have gathered the most prevalent ones here, accompanied by corresponding quotes. The quotes were taken directly from the survey write-in portions, and remain unedited and unmanipulated.

More

Bad Public Education

"My guidance counselor in high school told me not to apply to Brown..."
“I went to a large public high school where most of the resources were dedicated to getting students to graduate.”
“In my high school, almost no one really encouraged challenging ourselves to perform greater feats.” “Brown University was what they deemed a ‘dream’ school and said I could apply but I shouldn't put my hopes there...”
“My guidance counselor didn't think I'd get in...”
More

Parental Push

"My family values education above all else..."
“I always tried my best to excel at school to please my parents and make them proud.”
“Whenever [my mom] would lecture me, and have talk with me; she would always finish with- Once you finish college I will let you live.” “There was no question in my family about whether or not I would be going to college...”
More

Low Income

"My expected family contribution is unreasonable, and it has been a large stress for us. " “I didn't have many extracurriculars because I spent a lot of time in high school and middle school working at my parents' restaurant...”
“My parents have driven themselves to debt in order to ensure that my siblings and I could attend the best colleges we were accepted to.”
“[My parents] let me fill out the applications and write the essays, but when it came to actually paying to submit the application, they didn't want to pay for it, despite the fact that I did not have a job and could not pay for it myself.”
More

Low Self-Confidence

"I didn't have much confidence in my writing skills..." "I was not discouraged about the application process itself - more about my chances of getting in."
“I still fear the idea of [professors] viewing me incompetent or stupid because I'm asking them questions that have already been covered in class...”
“I find it hard, as a first gen, to feel confident to use these resources...” “ However, the few times I took a large lecture, especially in the math or science departments, the professors intimidated me quite a bit.”
"I spent most of my time at Brown being too scared to talk to professors. I felt that they would judge me for not being able to quickly grasp the subject material." “[I] didn't think I could get in. ”
More

Bad Admin

"I wish Brown would provide more advisors, because some freshman advisors were not that helpful." "This university is rotten."
“... I feel as though Paxson and the administration do a piss poor job of engaging minority alumni in Post-Brown discussions.”
“ This mentality, that you are unfit to study CS if you can't understand the materials immediately, was in fact, very much pervasive in the culture of the department as a whole.” “I didn't necessarily feel that [the deans] were invested in helping me - just that they didn't want me to slip”
More

Sense of Non-Belonging

"My floor basically seemed to comprise of athletes and white, brown haired girls from New Jersey. Their experiences seemed so vastly different from my own that it was incredibly difficult to relate to them." "I didn't feel like I particularly fit at Brown."
“The dominant culture here is definitely targeted towards students from families with a higher income level than mine, and that can often make me feel somewhat strange/alienated...”
“I have never felt welcome/like a part of the Brown student body. Basically, I've never felt like a Brown student.” “I don't think I fit in well at all...”
"I know very few students I can relate to, especially when it comes to having similar families or family struggles." “On a macrolevel, I feel and have felt incredibly out of place and unwelcome by the majority of spaces created/occupied by Brown students.”
More

Community

"My closest friends became my support network." "I found my community of students that helped me prosper and learn more about myself than I would have on my own."
“I felt that I had a real support network at Brown made up of peers who were from similar backgrounds as I, who shaped the lens through which I view my experiences now.” “They are not students, they are my family. ”
“I met a lot of friends at TWTP who are from similar backgrounds as I am from and this helped a lot my freshman year since I had people I could relate to.” “I have a small circle of friends I trust with a small network of other students I can relate to.”
"I feel like I have gotten the most support from fellow first generation students, fellow Filipinas, and fellow women of color. They have been some of the most important people in helping me navigate my time here, and not get so discouraged with the unfamiliarity and fast pace and various burdens (whether financial, academic, or otherwise) that I give up. " “Among students of color there are very cohesive communities and friend groups who promote and encourage success among one another.”
More

Brown Love

"Best decision of my life..." "Brown is the best university ever, hands down."
“I couldn't imagine being at any other college. Brown has opened up so many opportunities for me to rediscover my racial and ethnic identity, something that had caused a lot of problems in my life at home that I never realized because I denied those identities.” "I had a lot of fun, I was surrounded, supported, and encouraged by some of the amazing people I've ever met, and two years out, I feel satisfied that my education prepared me for the work that I want to be doing."
“I wouldn't have had a better [college learning experience] than at a school like Brown.” “I have been afforded tremendous opportunities here that I would not have been exposed to elsewhere.”
"I just know that even with its flaws, and my personal issues, I wouldn't want to be anywhere else... " “Overall, I'm more than satisfied with my experience at Brown. ”
