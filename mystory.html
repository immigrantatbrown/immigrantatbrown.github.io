My Story

I was born in Barranquilla, Colombia on May 20th, 1991. When I was 12 years old, my mother could no longer bear or economic difficulties and migrated to the United States to pave a better life for us. At 14, I chose to move to Miami, FL to follow her lead. Six months later I became undocumented. Living in fear for my immigration status through high school was very difficult. I feared deportation and the uncertainty of my future. College seemed very distant.

After graduating high school, I worked menial jobs for a year to save enough money to pay the out-of-state tuition fees (because of my status) at the local community college. There, I became involved in activism and excelled academically. I was no longer afraid.

When I first came to Brown the transitioning period was very difficult. The white privileged, elitist, heteronormative mainstream culture made me feel like I didn’t belong on campus and academically inadequate inside the classroom. Over the years, through involvement in student groups and activism, I met great people and found a welcoming community. Nevertheless, I never forgot how the first semesters were. I felt that this project was necessary to explore and share how other immigrant students at Brown feel, in hope to start a conversation of needs that aren’t being met and foster a stronger network of support for incoming students.
