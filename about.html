About This Project

This project was designed to gauge the experiences of first and second generation immigrant students at Brown University, an idea inspired by my own personal experience. I was interested in getting insight into the journeys of other individuals like me in an attempt to identify areas of need, opportunity, and strength. I set out to do so in ways I felt would encourage students to share their most honest, unfiltered thoughts and stories in order to gain a truly holistic view.

These methods included a 30 question anonymous survey, filled out by 71 students total. Survey questions ranged from the pragmatic to the profound. Write-in portions allowed an infinite amount of space for participants to reflect on their experiences and reveal as much or as little as they felt necessary. In addition to the survey, I sat down with 4 who agreed to do an on-camera interview. The flow of this two-person discussion format created a different space for insight into the lives of said individuals.

Also included on this site is a timeline which looks at the amazing contributions of immigrant students at Brown University throughout the course of history. By publishing the conclusions of my findings on the site, I hope to begin an ongoing conversation on the needs of these students that are not currently being met by the university. Beyond showcasing the hardships of the reality of student life for this community, I also hope to shed light on their resilience, their successes, and their remarkable strengths.

We are Brown students, and these are our stories.
